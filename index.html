Sasquatch

Size: 8-10 feet tall

The sasquatch is a hairy, upright-walking, ape-like being who reportedly dwells in remote, lush forests in mounrainous regions. The creature is known to leave behind large footprints, earning it the nickname Big Foot. Sightings have been reported across North America, strongly associated with the Pacific Northwest.

Tahoe Tessie

Size: 30-80 feet long

Tahoe Tessie is a large, aquatic creature that resides in North America's largest alpine lake, Lake Tahoe. It has a large, serpentine body "as wide across as a barrel,". Earliest sightings began in the mid-19th century by the Wahoe and Paiute tribes, stating that the creature resides in an underwater tunnel beneath Cave Rock.

Mothman

Size: 30-80 feet long

Mothman is a winged man reportedly seen in the Point Pleasant area of West Virginia. He is described as a large gray creature with glowing red eyes. Some speculate that Mothman was responsible for the collapse of Silver Bridgeon December 15, 1967 and the death of 46 people.

Jersey Devil

Size: 30-80 feet long

The Jersey devil inhabits the Pine Barrens of Southen New Jersey. The creature is described as a wyvern-like with the head of a goat, leather bat-like wings, horns, and small arms with clawed hands, cloven hooves, and a forked tail. There have been multiple reports of livestock killings attributed to this creature.

Chupacabra

Size: 3-4 feet tall

The chupacabra is a reptile-like creature, with scaly greenish-gray skin and sharp spines running down it's back. The first sighting was in Puerto Rico, followed by many more sightings throughout northern Mexico and the United States. It has a habit of attacking and drinking the blood of goats.

Wendigo

Size: 30-80 feet long

The wendigo wanders the northern forests of the Atlantic Coast and Great Lakes Region of the United States and Canada with an insatiable hunger for human flesh. The wendigo resembles a gaunt skeleton, with bloody, tattered lips, and emitting an odor of decay and decomposition.
